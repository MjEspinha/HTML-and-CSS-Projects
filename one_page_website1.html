Welcome to the Espinha Family tree!

Here you will find a little bit about the history of the 12th royal family of the Republic of Genoa!

The name Espinha was originally a Spanish family that moved to Portugal before the sixteenth century. This surname is of toponymic origin, being derived from the name of the place of residence of the initial bearer. In this instance, the surname is taken directly from the place-name Espina which is found in a number of Spanish regions including Galicia and the Vascongadas. Thus, the place-name signifies one from Espina. According to etymologists, the place-name Espina is derived from the word -Espino- which means hawthorn. For this reason, a hawthorn tree is shown on the coat of arms.
