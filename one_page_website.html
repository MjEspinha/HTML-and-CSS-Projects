Main History notes

The name Espinha was originally a Spanish family that moved to Portugal before the sixteenth century. This surname is of toponymic origin, being derived from the name of the place of residence of the initial bearer. In this instance, the surname is taken directly from the place-name Espina which is found in a number of Spanish regions including Galicia and the Vascongadas. Thus, the place-name signifies "one from Espina". According to etymologists, the place-name Espina is derived from the word "espino" which means "hawthorn." Thus, the place-name/surname Espina literally signifies "abundance of hawthorn." By extension, the name is also derived from the Latin word 'spinosus,' meaning thorny. For this reason, a hawthorn tree is shown on the coat of arms.
